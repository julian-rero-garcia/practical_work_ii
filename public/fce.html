Course Content

The course is structured to provide a comprehensive introduction to Computer Engineering through a combination of theoretical knowledge and practical skills. Its content is divided into two main parts: theoretical foundations and applied practice.

The theoretical component begins with an exploration of Computer Engineering in society, examining its historical development, its present role, and its future evolution. This includes an analysis of Computer Engineering as both an academic discipline and a profession, as well as the competencies expected of computer engineers. Special attention is given to ethical principles, professional responsibility, and deontological codes that guide engineering practice.

The course then addresses current paradigms and trends in computer systems and applications. Students are introduced to the structure of computer systems, encompassing hardware, software, operating systems, and networks. Emerging technological paradigms such as Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, Quantum Computing, Blockchain, Smart Cities, and the Internet of Things are presented to provide a broad view of contemporary and future developments in the field.

A further block is devoted to information and data. This includes the representation of information in digital systems, the organization and management of data, foundational concepts of Big Data, and the legal and ethical considerations associated with data processing and use.

The practical component of the course focuses on an introduction to software and web development. Students learn fundamental concepts of web technologies, version control systems such as Git and GitHub, and the creation and design of web pages using HTML5 and CSS3. These contents are applied through a project aimed at developing a web-based application related to the themes of the course.

Course Objectives

The primary objective of the course is to provide students with a global vision of Computer Engineering, enabling them to understand its purpose and its impact on society. The course seeks to encourage reflection on the role of technology in promoting human well-being and social development.

Another key objective is to establish the foundational principles that underpin technological progress. By acquiring a historical perspective on computing, students are expected to recognize the driving forces behind innovation and technological change.

The course also aims to highlight the importance of the human factor in engineering practice. It promotes ethical awareness, a vocation of service, and a commitment to the common good as essential elements of the professional identity of a computer engineer.

Additionally, the course seeks to familiarize students with the professional landscape of Computer Engineering. By presenting the various fields and career paths within the discipline, it encourages students to reflect on their own vocational interests and future professional development.

Finally, the course introduces current computing paradigms and basic technical skills, particularly in relation to data management and web development, as a foundation for more advanced studies.

Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of the course, students are expected to demonstrate a foundational understanding of computer systems, programming concepts, operating systems, and data-related technologies, and to recognize their application in engineering contexts.

Students should be able to describe the structure, organization, operation, and interconnection of computer systems, as well as explain the basic principles of hardware, software, and networks.

They are expected to correctly use technical terminology related to Computer Engineering and to identify key historical milestones in the evolution of computing, as well as analyze emerging and future trends in the discipline.

The course also aims for students to understand and apply mechanisms for representing and organizing numerical and non-numerical information within digital systems, taking into account legal and ethical considerations.

From a practical perspective, students should be capable of designing and developing a basic web-based application and of researching, documenting, and presenting a computing project clearly and effectively, both in written and oral form.
